Description about Project

This visualization shows monthly sales trends from 2014 to 2018, broken down by customer segment: Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office. The upper stacked area chart highlights that the Consumer segment consistently drives the highest sales across all time periods, followed by the Corporate and then the Home Office segments. The lower line chart aggregates total sales, showing a clear upward trend over time with noticeable seasonal peaks, particularly towards the end of each year. This indicates strong year-end sales performance, likely driven by holiday demand or end-of-year purchases.

Description about Project 2

This dashboard presents a global overview of key development indicators across four major sectors: fuel, communication, internet usage, and health. Fuel: The world map displays the percentage of merchandise exports made up by fuel exports for each country. Darker shades indicate higher dependency on fuel exports, with several countries in the Middle East, Africa, and parts of Asia showing significant reliance on fuel-related trade. Communication: The bar and line chart illustrates trends from 2008 to 2018. Fixed telephone subscriptions (orange bars) have steadily declined, while fixed broadband subscriptions (blue line) have shown a consistent increase, reflecting the global shift from traditional telephony to internet-based communication. Internet: The horizontal bar chart highlights the top countries with the highest percentage of population using the internet. Northern and Western European countries like Iceland, Norway, and Denmark lead the chart, showcasing widespread internet access. Korea, Rep. also ranks high, emphasizing strong digital infrastructure in Asia. Health: The bubble chart plots countries by the percentage of population aged 65 and older versus life expectancy at birth. Bubble sizes represent current health expenditure. Countries like Japan, Israel, and many in Europe show high life expectancy and aging populations, while others like Chad and Lesotho indicate lower life expectancy and minimal elderly populations. The chart offers insights into the correlation between aging, longevity, and healthcare investment globally.
